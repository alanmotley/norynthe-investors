Core Business Thesis

The Business Thesis

Norynthe sells access to the evidence behind independent AI evaluation.

The public trust score creates visibility. The paid product helps enterprises understand, compare, document, and defend AI-related decisions.

The business model depends on turning the current working MVP and report surfaces into repeatable evaluation infrastructure, strengthened by upgraded compute and larger-model testing.

As AI adoption grows, buyers need external evaluation for procurement, governance, and risk review. The first commercial wedge is enterprise CTO and procurement teams evaluating AI systems before adoption, expansion, or vendor selection. Government and institutional verification become expansion paths after the enterprise workflow is proven.

Who Pays First

Enterprise adoption review is the first wedge.

Norynthe should first sell where model choice already creates budget pressure: CTO, procurement, risk, governance, and AI program teams that need independent evidence before adopting, expanding, or defending AI systems.

CTO / AI Program Owners Need defensible model comparison before choosing platforms, agents, or deployment patterns.
Procurement Teams Need a buyer-readable record that moves beyond vendor claims and demo polish.
Risk And Governance Need evidence trails, flags, score context, and review posture for internal controls.
Compliance-Adjacent Review Need decision support before policy, audit, or regulated-use review becomes formal.
Regulated Enterprise Expands after the enterprise workflow proves report usefulness and repeatable evaluation value.
Government / Public Sector Later expansion path where defensible procurement and independent AI review become institutional needs.
