Investor diligence snapshot

Where the company stands now.

A concise readout of current proof, the $2M unlock, commercial wedge, defensibility, and capital milestones without exposing proprietary benchmark mechanics.

Current proof

More than a concept deck.

Norynthe has an investor packet, working console surface, sample report system, financial model, raise plan, founder memo, enterprise use cases, and methodology paper.

$2M unlock

From founder-built proof to repeatable trust lab.

The raise funds benchmark v1, scoring repeatability, paid pilot packaging, customer report infrastructure, owned compute, and the validation loop needed to turn the current review surface into an operating AI trust lab.

Commercial wedge

Enterprise CTO and procurement first.

The first wedge is enterprise AI evaluation for CTO, procurement, risk, and governance teams that need independent evidence before adopting, comparing, or expanding AI systems. Government and institutional review are expansion paths, not the first dependency.

Next proof

Validation, repeatability, and paid pilots.

The next work centers on scoring consistency across repeated runs, report usefulness with early buyers, and converting customer-facing evaluations into paid pilot format.

Defensibility

An evolving benchmark system, not a static prompt set.

The benchmark becomes harder to replicate because it compounds rubric governance, benchmark versioning, evaluator contracts, calibration history, proprietary scoring records, and market trust over time. Trademark can protect the public benchmark identity and naming, while the deeper moat is governance, data, and iteration.

Why Norynthe

Independent evaluation records beyond compliance checklists.

Model providers are conflicted, consulting firms are not infrastructure-native, and many GRC platforms begin with policy controls rather than model behavior. Norynthe starts from independent evaluation records: benchmark-owned, rubric-governed, evidence-backed, and designed for comparison across vendors.
